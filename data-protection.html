Who is the organisation collecting your data?

The organisation collecting your data is the initiative London Maths Outreach (LMO). LMO is a small unregistered charity and run by mathematics PhD students based in London. The individual members of our initiative are listed on the About Us page. Submitted data are controlled by Todd Schmid (E-Mail: todd.schmid.19@ucl.ac.uk) and Ashwin Iyengar (E-Mail: a.iyengar.17@ucl.ac.uk). Data is with other parties if necessary to run the sessions. When required by the universities, the data is shared with the universities. The contact information of the data protection officers at the universities follows:

  • University College London: Lee Shailer, data-protection@ucl.ac.uk, +44 (0)20 3108 8726
  • King’s College London: Albert Chan, info-compliance@kcl.ac.uk
  • Imperial College London: Robert Scott, Robert.scott@imperial.ac.uk, +44(0) 20 7594 3502

Data is also shared with teachers to the extent that is necessary to run the classes for the duration of their teaching. Contact information of individual teachers can be found on the Courses page.

Why will we be using your personal data?

We are using your personal data to communicate with you about our outreach sessions. This includes sharing information such as date, location, and possible disruptions of the course schedule. We are also using your data to understand what effect our taster sessions and advertisement posters prior to the the start of the programme had. The information about your mathematical experience and interests is used by the teachers to help them prepare their classes. Guardian/parental contact information is used to inform them in case of an emergency involving their child. If asked to do so by parent/guardian and child we also share the enrollment/un-enrollment of children with their schools.

What are the categories of personal data concerned?

We only collect personal data when you share them directly with us through the contact form on our website, personally, via email, or a “parental information form” that has been given to students in the first week of the programme. The categories of personal data concerned is the category of data that you are sharing directly with us.

What is the legal justification for processing your data?

We are providing a task in the public interest linked to the core purpose “Education” of the supporting universities.

For how long will your data be kept?

Your data will be kept as long as you are a student in our programme. The expected time that you stay in our programme is from the moment you register until the end of your thirteenth school year.

Who else might receive your data?

We store our data on servers of University College London. The service (UCL OneDrive) claims to be encrypted, using the encryption tools provided by the Microsoft platform.

Will your personal data be transferred to a recipient outside the EU?

Not as long the UK remains in the EU.

Information about your rights

Under certain circumstances, you may have the following rights in relation to your personal data:

  • Right 1: A right to access personal data held by us about you.
  • Right 2: A right to require us to rectify any inaccurate personal data held by us about you.
  • Right 3: A right to require us to erase personal data held by us about you. This right will only apply where, for example, we no longer need to use the personal data to achieve the purpose we collected it for; or where you withdraw your consent if we are using your personal data based on your consent; or where you object to the way we process your data (in line with Right 6 below).
  • Right 4: A right to restrict our processing of personal data held by us about you. This right will only apply where, for example, you dispute the accuracy of the personal data held by us; or where you would have the right to require us to erase the personal data but would prefer that our processing is restricted instead; or where we no longer need to use the personal data to achieve the purpose we collected it for, but we require the data for the purposes of dealing with legal claims.
  • Right 5: A right to receive personal data, which you have provided to us, in a structured, commonly used and machine readable format. You also have the right to require us to transfer this personal data to another organisation.
  • Right 6: A right to object to our processing of personal data held by us about you.
  • Right 7: A right to withdraw your consent, where we are relying on it to use your personal data.
  • Right 8: A right to ask us not to use information about you in a way that allows computers to make decisions about you and ask us to stop.
